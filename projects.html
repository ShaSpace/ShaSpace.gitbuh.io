Plant Monitor

The Plant Monitor, is a project from my Intro to Embedded Engineering class. It is a 3D printed plant box with sensors and a PCB embedded within it. The purpose of the monitor is alert the plant owner when the plant is not receiving the correct amount of light, if it is too hot or cold, and the soil moisture level. It includes a light sensor, temperature sensor, and a moisture sensor. I handled the software side of configuring our microcontroller to the external sensors. This was done with Code Composer using C#, and my partner did the PCB design in Altium.

The CAD and 3D printing was done by Jonathan Gallinaro. Check him out here!

Geo-Technical Lab Simulation

The geo-technical lab simulation is a part of a project of creating an app for students interested in geo-technical engineering. I worked on this simulation during my third year of undergrad. This simulation helps provide students an example of a water cleaning technique often used in the field, through Unity's 2D gaming engine. Users can adjust the lab environment to change the outcome of the solution, this way it gives them a base understanding of the technique without the lab equipment. The modeling was done by mechanical engineering students through Blender, while I handled the coding and the placement through Unity.

Blender Donut

This is a spinning 3D modeled donut with icing and sprinkles. I followed Blenger Guru's tutorials on YouTube to recreate his donut, and conduct a personal study on Blender's modeling capabilities. Everything was done through blender including: the modeling, the texturing, the animating, the rendering, and the sequencing of frames. It took me 3-4 weeks to complete this tutorial, due to the number and length and videos, and to reinforce my understanding of the techniques. This tutorial sparked my interest in 3D modeling as a whole.

IoT Project

This Internet of Things, IoT, project was completed my sophomore year of undergrad during my Sophomore Engineering Clinic II class. It is a simple circut connecting a couple sensors to an Arduino board, along with a WiFi module, to send the data to ThingsSpeak.com. The two sensors used were a temperature sensor, and photoresistor. As the light and temperature of the environment changes, the graphs on the site reflected as such. I was tasked with configuring the board to external parameter connections through Arduino's IDE, connecting everything through bread board, and making sure the data reached ThingsSpeak.

Blender Brownie

This is a spinning 3D model of a brownie with walnuts on a plate. After my blender donut study, I decided to apply what I learned to recreate one of my favorite desserts. The modeling, texturing, animating, sequencing, lighting, and video editing is all done through Blender. I put my own spin on it, and had the spin animation be a bit different than the tutorial. This project has inspired me to start designing my own blender animations and environment simulations to have another way of communicating my concepts and ideas.

4 Bit Odd Parity VLSI Circuit

The above gif shows the different steps of microchip fabrication of a 4-bit odd parity circuit. This circuit has various applications, but my favorite would be for encryption purposes. It can take a four bit input, check the numer of 1s within the sequence, and then based on the output the user or the next circuit can make a decision. All of the calculations were done by hand with the help of my TI-Nspire calculator, and the design was replicated and simulated in Cadence Virtuoso. Due to the application being hosted on a server, I had to get familiar with git commands in order to access my files and the applications. This project was my major senior project for my Very Large Scale Integration class.
